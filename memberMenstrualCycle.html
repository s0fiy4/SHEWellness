Understanding Your Menstrual Cycle

The menstrual cycle is a natural process that occurs in women's bodies, typically lasting between 21 to 35 days, although it can vary among individuals. It involves hormonal changes that prepare the uterus for potential pregnancy each month.

Key aspects of the menstrual cycle:

  • Phases of the Menstrual Cycle: The cycle includes the follicular phase, ovulation, and the luteal phase. Understanding these phases helps women track their fertility and understand their body's hormonal rhythm.
  • Managing Menstrual Health: Proper menstrual hygiene practices, such as using sanitary products and maintaining cleanliness, are crucial to prevent infections and maintain comfort during menstruation.
  • Common Menstrual Disorders: Disorders like dysmenorrhea (painful periods), menorrhagia (heavy menstrual bleeding), and amenorrhea (absence of periods) can impact women's health and may require medical attention.
  • Nutrition and Lifestyle Tips: Eating a balanced diet rich in iron and maintaining regular exercise can help alleviate menstrual symptoms and promote overall well-being throughout the cycle.
